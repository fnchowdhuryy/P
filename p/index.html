Welcome to my Blog

Hidden in the north eastern part of Bangladesh is Sylhet, a region that would be of interest to those with an adventurous thirst for nature. Sylhet is famous for its green tea estates, valleys and rivers that are so clear that tourists coming here can choose from many beautiful places. One very magical place is Ratargul Swamp Forest; an unusual freshwater swamp where one can take a peaceful boat ride through sunken trees producing an eerie like experience. Another must visit location is Jaflong where you can take stunning pictures because it stands by the Piyain River which gives you a pristine view of Meghalaya hills at distance. What makes Sylhet so attractive is not only its beauty but also the calmness associated with it, hence making it a perfect haven for people who want to get out of citiesâ€™ hustle and bustle.

Ratargul

Ratalgul: Ratargul is the only 'freshwater swamp forest' in our country. The distance from Sylhet to this country's only recognized swamp forest is about 20 kilometers. The location of this water forest is Khadimnagar union of Sadar Upazila of Sylhet district and Fatehpur union of bordering Upazila Gowainghat.

Shapla bill

Shapla Bill in Sylhet (Dibir Haor) Winter is the time to visit Shapla Bill in Sylhet. From late December, you can experience the beautiful Shapla flowers along with thousands of migratory birds (from Eastern Siberia). In the rainy season, you will miss the birds.

Srimangal Tea Garden

Srimangal, the tea capital of Bangladesh, having most of the tea gardens of the country, is called "The land of two leaves and a bud". The largest tea garden in the world is situated here. There are terraced tea gardens and pineapple, rubber, and lemon plantation.

Bisnakandi

Bisnakandi, conveniently situated near Sylhet on the India-Bangladesh, is a combination of green hills, clouds, a variety of stones and crystal-clear waters flowing from the north-eastern Meghalaya Mountains. Visitors can lie down on the rocks and relax or go for a peaceful swim.

HumHum waterfall

Hum Hum waterfall is situated in Razkandi reserve forest in Maulvibazar District. It was discovered in 2009. It is actually a place where you can find the real taste of adventure. The height of the fall is about 135-160 feet.
